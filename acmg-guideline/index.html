ACMG GUIDE

개요

이 문서는 ACMG/AMP germline sequence variant interpretation을 처음 보는 사람이 큰 그림을 잡을 수 있도록 정리한 한글 개요다. 목표는 "공식 기준", "최신 적용 guidance", "유전자/질환별 specification"을 섞지 않고 이해하는 것이다.

확인 기준일: 2026-05-10.

1. 한 문장 요약

ACMG/AMP 변이 해석은 한 germline sequence variant를 Pathogenic, Likely pathogenic, VUS, Likely benign, Benign 중 하나로 분류하기 위해 여러 종류의 증거를 정해진 강도로 조합하는 체계다.

2. 가장 먼저 알아야 할 세 층

층 무엇인가 지금 어떻게 쓰나 헷갈리기 쉬운 점
2015 ACMG/AMP guideline Richards et al., 2015 (Genetics in Medicine)의 공식 출판 기준선 germline sequence variant 해석의 기본 프레임 오래됐지만 현재 공개적으로 확인 가능한 공식 기준선이다
ClinGen Variant Classification Guidance 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline criteria를 더 일관되게 적용하기 위한 ClinGen 권고 모음 PVS1, PM2, PS2/PM6, PP3/BP4, PS3/BS3 등 criteria별 세부 적용 확인 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 전체를 대체한 새 guideline이 아니다
ClinGen VCEP/CSpec 특정 gene/disease에 맞춘 ACMG/AMP criteria specification 해당 유전자와 질환 조합이 맞을 때 우선 확인 모든 변이에 일반화하면 안 된다

3. ACMG/AMP가 실제로 묻는 질문

ACMG/AMP classification은 "이 환자가 이 병을 갖고 있는가?"를 자동으로 판정하는 도구가 아니다. 더 정확한 질문은 다음과 같다.

이 특정 variant가, 특정 gene-disease context에서, 질병 원인으로 볼 수 있을 만큼 충분한 증거를 갖고 있는가?

따라서 판정 전에 세 가지를 고정해야 한다.

먼저 고정할 것 예시 왜 중요한가
Variant identity transcript, cDNA, protein notation 같은 gene 안에서도 variant마다 증거가 다르다
Gene-disease context SCN1A와 developmental epileptic encephalopathy 같은 gene도 질환별 mechanism이 다를 수 있다
Inheritance model autosomal dominant, autosomal recessive, X-linked population frequency, segregation, de novo evidence 해석이 달라진다

이 세 가지가 불분명하면 evidence code를 붙여도 논리적으로 흔들린다.

4. 다섯 가지 최종 분류

분류 한글 설명 실무적 의미
Pathogenic 병적 변이 질병 원인으로 볼 증거가 충분함
Likely pathogenic 병적 가능성이 높은 변이 질병 원인일 가능성이 높지만 pathogenic만큼 확정적이지는 않음
VUS 임상적 의미 불명 변이 증거가 부족하거나 충돌함. 진단적 결론으로 과잉해석하면 안 됨
Likely benign 양성 가능성이 높은 변이 질병 원인이 아닐 가능성이 높음
Benign 양성 변이 질병 원인이 아니라고 볼 증거가 충분함

가장 중요한 메시지는 VUS다. VUS는 "약한 양성"이나 "수상한 변이"가 아니라 "현재 증거로는 판단 보류"라는 뜻이다.

5. Evidence code의 기본 구조

ACMG/AMP evidence code는 pathogenic 방향과 benign 방향으로 나뉜다.

방향 강도 코드 예시 직관적 의미
Pathogenic Very Strong PVS1 질병 mechanism과 맞는 loss-of-function 등 매우 강한 증거
Pathogenic Strong PS1, PS2, PS3, PS4 같은 amino acid change, confirmed de novo, 기능실험, case enrichment 등
Pathogenic Moderate PM1-PM6 hotspot, rarity, in trans, protein length change 등
Pathogenic Supporting PP1-PP5 segregation, computational evidence, phenotype specificity 등
Benign Stand-alone BA1 너무 흔해서 pathogenic일 수 없음
Benign Strong BS1-BS4 빈도 높음, 건강 성인 관찰, 기능 정상, non-segregation 등
Benign Supporting BP1-BP7 benign 방향의 약한 보조 증거

이 코드는 단순 점수표가 아니다. 각 criteria가 정말 적용 가능한지, 서로 독립적인지, 최신 ClinGen guidance에서 strength가 바뀌었는지 확인해야 한다.

28개 criteria의 전체 표, 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 조합 규칙, criterion별 상세 설명, PP3/BP4 cutoff 예시는 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline Criteria와 이후 업데이트에 따로 정리했다. 이 개요에서 큰 그림을 먼저 잡고, 이어지는 criteria 문서에서 실제 적용 기준을 하나씩 확인하면 된다.

6. 실무적 판정 흐름

ACMG/AMP 변이 해석을 교육용으로 단순화하면 다음 흐름이다.

1. variant를 정확히 정의한다
2. gene-disease validity와 inheritance를 확인한다
3. population frequency로 benign 가능성을 먼저 확인한다
4. variant consequence와 disease mechanism을 본다
5. computational, functional, segregation, de novo, phenotype evidence를 모은다
6. ACMG/AMP criteria와 evidence strength를 붙인다
7. ClinGen general/criterion-specific guidance와 VCEP/CSpec이 있는지 확인한다
8. scope가 맞는 VCEP/CSpec이 있으면 해당 specification을 우선 적용한다
9. evidence 조합으로 5-tier classification을 정한다
10. VUS이면 무엇이 부족한지 명시한다

Benign evidence를 먼저 보는 이유는 단순하다. 질환 빈도와 penetrance를 고려했을 때 너무 흔한 variant는 pathogenic일 가능성이 낮기 때문이다.

7. 독자가 혼동하기 쉬운 지점

혼동 바로잡기
최신 파일이면 공식 guideline이다 최신 draft와 공식 final은 다르다
ACMG는 하나의 문서만 뜻한다 baseline guideline, guidance, specification, draft standard가 구분된다
gene-level evidence가 variant-level evidence다 gene-disease validity와 variant pathogenicity는 다르다
in silico 결과가 충분하면 pathogenic이다 computational evidence는 보통 supporting level이다
VUS는 거의 pathogenic이다 VUS는 판단 보류다
VCEP rule은 모든 gene에 적용된다 지정된 gene/disease 범위 안에서만 쓴다

주요 Evidence Domain

1. Population Frequency

질문: 이 variant가 질환 원인이라고 보기에는 너무 흔하지 않은가?

확인할 것:

  • gnomAD 등 population database에서 allele frequency가 얼마인가?
  • ancestry가 맞는 population에서 확인했는가?
  • 질환 유병률, penetrance, inheritance model을 고려했을 때 빈도가 허용 가능한가?
  • BA1 또는 BS1 같은 benign evidence에 해당하는가?
  • 단순히 "없다"는 이유로 pathogenic evidence를 과하게 주고 있지는 않은가?

주의: gnomAD에 없다는 사실은 대개 강한 pathogenic evidence가 아니다. 매우 드문 variant는 흔히 관찰된다.

2. Variant Consequence와 Gene Mechanism

질문: variant가 단백질이나 splicing에 어떤 영향을 줄 것으로 예상되는가?

예시:

  • nonsense 또는 frameshift
  • canonical splice donor/acceptor
  • missense
  • synonymous with possible splicing effect
  • in-frame insertion/deletion
  • start-loss 또는 stop-loss

중요한 점은 gene mechanism이다. 예를 들어 truncating variant라도 해당 gene-disease에서 loss-of-function이 확립된 disease mechanism이어야 PVS1을 강하게 적용할 수 있다.

주의: 모든 truncating variant에 자동으로 PVS1을 붙이면 안 된다. transcript relevance, nonsense-mediated decay, exon 위치, gene mechanism을 확인해야 한다.

3. Computational Prediction

질문: in silico predictor들이 variant의 영향을 어떤 방향으로 지지하는가?

예시:

  • missense predictor: REVEL, BayesDel, AlphaMissense 등
  • splicing predictor: SpliceAI 등
  • conservation, protein domain, structural context

주의: computational evidence는 보통 supporting level이다. 서로 비슷한 predictor들을 여러 개 더해 독립 증거처럼 세면 안 된다.

4. Functional Evidence

질문: 기능실험이 질환 관련 기능 손상 또는 정상 기능을 설득력 있게 보여주는가?

확인할 것:

  • assay가 disease mechanism과 직접 관련 있는가?
  • known pathogenic/benign control로 calibration 되었는가?
  • threshold가 재현 가능하고 명확한가?
  • PS3 또는 BS3로 쓸 만큼 강한가, 아니면 supporting 수준인가?

주의: wet-lab 결과라고 항상 strong evidence는 아니다. assay validity가 핵심이다.

5. Segregation과 De Novo Evidence

질문: variant가 가족 내 질환과 함께 움직이는가, 또는 affected proband에서 de novo로 발생했는가?

확인할 것:

  • parentage가 확인되었는가?
  • phenotype이 gene-disease와 잘 맞는가?
  • penetrance와 age-related expression을 고려했는가?
  • unaffected carrier가 있는가?
  • 다른 pathogenic variant나 alternate cause가 있는가?

주의: de novo라는 말만으로 충분하지 않다. parentage, phenotype specificity, alternate cause를 확인해야 한다.

6. Case Observation과 Database Evidence

질문: 이 variant가 환자군, ClinVar, 문헌, expert panel에서 어떻게 보고되었는가?

확인할 것:

  • ClinVar review status와 submitter confidence
  • ClinGen expert panel classification 여부
  • phenotype과 transcript 일치 여부
  • 보고 시점과 최신 reclassification 여부
  • 같은 variant인지, 같은 amino acid change인지, 같은 gene만 같은 것인지

주의: ClinVar에 pathogenic이라고 적혀 있다고 그대로 복사하면 안 된다. evidence detail과 review status를 확인해야 한다.

Criteria 조합의 직관

ACMG/AMP는 evidence code 조합으로 최종 분류를 만든다. 교육용으로는 다음 원칙을 먼저 이해하면 된다.

  • 강한 pathogenic evidence가 충분히 모이면 Pathogenic 또는 Likely pathogenic으로 간다.
  • 강한 benign evidence가 충분하면 Benign 또는 Likely benign으로 간다.
  • 증거가 부족하거나 서로 충돌하면 대부분 VUS에 남는다.
  • BA1처럼 stand-alone benign evidence가 있으면 약한 pathogenic signal보다 우선한다.
  • 같은 정보에서 나온 evidence를 여러 번 세면 안 된다.

HTML 페이지에서는 evidence block이 쌓여 final class로 이동하는 구조를 보여주되, "자동 진단 계산기"처럼 보이지 않게 해야 한다.

ClinGen은 어디에 들어가나

ClinGen은 2015 ACMG/AMP framework를 실제로 더 일관되게 적용하기 위한 guidance와 specification을 제공한다.

실무적으로는 다음 순서가 안전하다.

  1. Variant identity, gene-disease context, inheritance model을 먼저 고정한다.
  2. 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline으로 evidence code와 5-tier classification의 큰 구조를 잡는다.
  3. ClinGen Variant Classification Guidance에서 general recommendation과 criterion-specific recommendation을 확인한다.
  4. 해당 gene/disease에 승인된 VCEP/CSpec specification이 있는지 확인한다.
  5. scope가 맞으면 gene/disease-specific VCEP/CSpec rule을 일반 ClinGen guidance보다 우선 적용한다.
  6. 각 criterion별 primary evidence를 기록하고, strength modification과 version/date를 남긴다.
  7. 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 조합 규칙 또는 승인된 VCEP-specific 조합 규칙으로 최종 tier를 산출한다.

이 순서는 다음 소스에서 온 operational synthesis다.

  • Richards et al., 2015 (Genetics in Medicine): 기본 evidence criteria와 5-tier rule-based framework.
  • ClinGen Variant Classification Guidance: ACMG/AMP criteria를 쓰기 위한 general recommendation, criterion-specific recommendation, ClinGen-endorsed recommendation 목록.
  • ClinGen CSpec Registry: ClinGen VCEP과 biocurator가 사용하는 gene/disease-specific criteria specification.
  • ClinGen General Sequence Variant Curation Process SOP v3.2: 각 criterion의 evidence를 확인하고 평가한 뒤 ACMG/AMP 조합 규칙으로 provisional classification을 산출하는 VCI workflow.

이 문서는 germline sequence variant 해석에서 가장 기본이 되는 Richards et al., 2015 (Genetics in Medicine)의 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 28개 evidence criteria를 한글로 정리한 핵심 노트다. 목표는 다음 세 가지다.

  1. 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 공식 기준선에서 각 criterion이 무엇을 뜻하는지 이해한다.
  2. criterion을 적용하기 전에 확인해야 할 조건과 흔한 실수를 정리한다.
  3. 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 이후 ClinGen guidance가 어떤 criterion을 어떻게 구체화했는지 구분한다.

중요한 결론부터 말하면, 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline은 아직 공개적으로 확인 가능한 공식 기준선이다. ClinGen Variant Classification Guidance는 이 기준을 대체하는 새 최종 guideline이 아니라, 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline criteria를 더 일관되게 쓰기 위한 권고 모음이다.

VCEP/CSpec specification gene/disease-specific 기준 해당 gene-disease 조합에만 적용 CSpec Registry

2. 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline은 rule-based framework다

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline은 단순한 점수 합산표가 아니라 rule-based 조합 체계다. 각 evidence criterion은 pathogenic 방향 또는 benign 방향의 근거이며, 근거 강도는 Very Strong, Strong, Moderate, Supporting, Stand-alone으로 나뉜다. 최종 분류는 정해진 조합 규칙으로 결정된다.

현재 권고안과 차기 적용 방향은 점수 기반 framework를 더 많이 사용한다. Tavtigian et al., 2020 (Human Mutation)은 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline의 evidence strength를 Bayesian odds에 비례하는 point system으로 추상화할 수 있다고 설명했고, ClinGen Variant Classification Guidance는 이 논문을 ClinGen-endorsed recommendation으로 제시한다. Bergquist et al., 2025 (Genetics in Medicine) Table 1 footnote도 ACMG/AMP guideline이 point-based system으로 전환될 것으로 예상된다고 명시한다.

분류 전 반드시 고정해야 할 세 가지가 있다.

먼저 고정할 것 예시 이유
Variant identity transcript, cDNA, protein, genome build 같은 variant인지 아닌지 판단해야 함
Gene-disease context SCN1A - developmental epileptic encephalopathy 같은 gene도 질환별 mechanism이 다를 수 있음
Inheritance model AD, AR, X-linked population frequency, segregation, de novo, phase 해석이 달라짐

기본 decision tree는 다음과 같다.

Variant 발견
  -> HGVS와 transcript를 확정한다
  -> gene-disease validity와 inheritance를 확인한다
  -> population frequency로 BA1/BS1/BS2 가능성을 먼저 확인한다
  -> variant consequence와 disease mechanism을 연결한다
  -> PVS1, PM4, BP3, BP7 등 consequence 기반 criteria를 검토한다
  -> functional, computational, segregation, de novo, phenotype evidence를 모은다
  -> 각 criterion의 적용 가능성과 evidence independence를 확인한다
  -> ClinGen guidance와 VCEP/CSpec이 있으면 strength와 cutoff를 조정한다
  -> 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 조합 규칙 또는 승인된 gene-specific rule로 최종 tier를 결정한다

5. Criteria별 상세 설명

5.1 PVS1: LOF가 disease mechanism인 gene의 null variant

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: nonsense, frameshift, canonical +/-1 or 2 splice site, initiation codon, single/multiexon deletion 같은 null variant가 있고, 해당 gene에서 loss-of-function이 알려진 disease mechanism일 때 적용한다.

적용 전에 확인할 것:

  • 해당 gene-disease에서 LOF가 확립된 disease mechanism인가?
  • variant가 실제로 nonsense-mediated decay를 유도할 위치인가?
  • 마지막 exon 또는 penultimate exon의 마지막 50 bp 근처라 NMD를 피할 가능성은 없는가?
  • splice variant가 exon skipping을 만들더라도 in-frame으로 critical domain이 보존되는가?
  • clinically relevant transcript에서 해석하고 있는가?
  • dominant-negative 또는 gain-of-function gene인데 truncating을 LOF처럼 오해하고 있지는 않은가?

ClinGen 업데이트: PVS1은 2018 ClinGen SVI 권고에서 가장 많이 구체화된 criterion 중 하나다. 핵심은 PVS1을 항상 Very Strong으로 고정하지 않고 variant type, NMD 예측, exon 위치, critical region, transcript relevance, gene mechanism에 따라 PVS1, PVS1_Strong, PVS1_Moderate, PVS1_Supporting, not met로 낮출 수 있다는 점이다.

간단한 decision tree:

Null-like variant인가?
  아니오 -> PVS1 검토 대상 아님
  예 -> 해당 gene-disease에서 LOF가 established mechanism인가?
        아니오 -> PVS1 적용하지 않음
        예 -> variant가 NMD를 유도하거나 critical protein loss를 만들 가능성이 높은가?
              예 -> PVS1 또는 PVS1_Strong
              불확실 -> PVS1_Moderate 또는 PVS1_Supporting
              아니오 -> PVS1 적용하지 않거나 다른 criterion 검토

5.2 PS1: 같은 amino acid change

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: nucleotide change는 달라도, 단백질 수준에서 이미 pathogenic으로 확립된 variant와 같은 amino acid change이면 적용한다.

예: 기존 p.Val600Glu가 pathogenic이고 다른 nucleotide change로 같은 p.Val600Glu가 생기면 PS1을 검토한다.

확인할 것:

  • 비교 대상 variant가 truly pathogenic 또는 likely pathogenic인지 확인한다.
  • 같은 amino acid change라도 splicing impact가 다를 수 있다.
  • transcript와 codon context가 같은지 확인한다.
  • 비교 대상이 같은 residue의 다른 amino acid change라면 PS1이 아니라 PM5 검토 대상이다.

Splicing 업데이트: ClinGen SVI splicing guidance는 predicted/observed RNA effect가 같을 때 PS1을 어떻게 생각할지 논의한다. 즉, 단순 protein-level sameness만이 아니라 splicing effect의 동등성을 따져야 하는 상황이 있다.

5.3 PS2: confirmed de novo

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: 환자가 해당 disease를 갖고 있고 가족력이 없으며, maternity와 paternity가 모두 확인된 de novo variant일 때 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • 부모 sample에서 variant가 없음을 확인했는가?
  • parental relationship이 확인되었는가?
  • phenotype이 gene-disease와 일치하는가?
  • mosaicism, nonpaternity, egg donation, embryo transfer 같은 가능성을 고려했는가?
  • 같은 variant의 독립 de novo observation이 여러 건 있는가?

ClinGen 업데이트: PS2/PM6는 2018 권고와 2021 v1.1 업데이트에서 point-based 방식으로 구체화되었다. de novo 한 건마다 phenotype specificity와 confirmed/assumed parental relationship에 따라 점수를 부여하고, 총점에 따라 Supporting, Moderate, Strong, Very Strong으로 strength를 정한다.

간단한 decision tree:

proband phenotype이 gene-disease와 맞는가?
  아니오 -> PS2/PM6 사용하지 않음
  예 -> 부모가 variant-negative인가?
        아니오 또는 부모 미검사 -> de novo evidence 없음
        예 -> parental relationship confirmed인가?
              예 -> PS2 계열
              아니오 -> PM6 계열
        이후 phenotype specificity와 독립 observation 수로 strength 결정

5.4 PS3: 기능실험이 damaging effect를 지지

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: well-established in vitro 또는 in vivo functional study가 gene 또는 gene product에 damaging effect를 지지할 때 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • assay가 disease mechanism을 직접 측정하는가?
  • variant effect가 clinical pathogenicity와 연결되는가?
  • known pathogenic/benign controls가 충분한가?
  • assay threshold가 사전에 정의되었고 재현 가능한가?
  • 실험 결과가 단백질 기능, splicing, expression, localization 중 무엇을 측정하는가?

ClinGen 업데이트: Brnich et al., 2019 (Genome Medicine) PS3/BS3 guidance는 기능실험을 네 단계로 평가하도록 권고한다. 질병 mechanism 정의, assay class 적합성, assay instance validation, individual variant 적용을 순서대로 확인한다. 충분한 control과 statistical framework가 없으면 Strong을 그대로 쓰지 않고 Moderate 또는 Supporting으로 낮춰야 한다.

5.5 PS4: affected cases에서 유의한 enrichment

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: affected individuals에서 variant prevalence가 controls보다 유의하게 높을 때 적용한다. 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline은 relative risk 또는 odds ratio가 5보다 크고 confidence interval이 1을 포함하지 않는 상황을 strong evidence 예로 든다. 매우 드문 variant에서는 여러 unrelated patients에서 반복 관찰되고 controls에서 없다는 근거를 moderate evidence로 쓸 수 있다고 설명한다.

확인할 것:

  • case와 control ascertainment가 맞는가?
  • ancestry matching이 되었는가?
  • phenotype과 disease definition이 일관적인가?
  • 같은 환자나 같은 family를 중복 count하지 않았는가?
  • publication bias와 ClinVar 중복 보고를 확인했는가?

주의: "환자 한 명에서 보였다"는 사실은 PS4가 아니다. 통계적 비교 또는 반복 독립 관찰이 필요하다.

5.6 PM1: mutational hotspot 또는 critical functional domain

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: benign variation이 없는 mutational hot spot 또는 well-established critical functional domain에 위치한 variant일 때 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • domain이 functional assay, structural biology, curated disease mechanism으로 확립되었는가?
  • 같은 domain에 benign missense가 많이 존재하지 않는가?
  • residue-level 또는 domain-level constraint가 disease와 연결되는가?
  • hotspot이라는 말이 단순히 변이가 많이 보고된 위치라는 뜻으로 오용되지 않는가?

주의: protein domain database에 이름이 있다는 것만으로 PM1을 적용하면 안 된다.

5.7 PM2: population controls에서 absent 또는 very rare

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: Exome Sequencing Project, 1000 Genomes, ExAC 같은 controls에서 absent이거나 recessive disorder에서는 extremely low frequency일 때 적용한다.

현재 권고안:

  • 현재 실무에서는 gnomAD 등 더 큰 population database를 함께 본다.
  • ancestry-specific allele frequency를 확인한다.
  • coverage, low complexity, CNV/indel calling limitation을 확인한다.
  • disease prevalence, penetrance, inheritance, allelic heterogeneity를 고려한다.

ClinGen 업데이트: 2020 SVI PM2 권고는 PM2를 기본적으로 Moderate에서 Supporting으로 낮추는 것을 제안했다. 이유는 absence/rarity alone이 pathogenicity의 moderate odds를 충분히 제공하지 못한다는 점이다. 따라서 많은 ClinGen/VCEP specification에서는 PM2_Supporting을 사용한다.

주의: "gnomAD에 없다"는 사실은 흔히 보이는 rare variant 현상이다. 단독으로 pathogenic 결론을 만들면 안 된다.

5.8 PM3: recessive disorder에서 trans

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: recessive disorder에서 평가 중인 variant가 다른 pathogenic variant와 trans로 관찰될 때 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • phase가 부모 또는 offspring testing으로 확인되었는가?
  • 반대 allele variant가 pathogenic 또는 likely pathogenic인가?
  • affected patient에서 관찰되었는가?
  • homozygous observation인지 compound heterozygous인지 구분한다.
  • 두 variant가 같은 gene-disease mechanism에 맞는가?

ClinGen 업데이트: 2019 SVI PM3 권고는 point-based system을 제안했다. confirmed in trans, phase unknown, homozygous occurrence, 반대 allele variant의 classification에 따라 proband별 점수를 부여하고 총점으로 PM3_Supporting, PM3, PM3_Strong, PM3_VeryStrong을 결정한다.

5.9 PM4: protein length change

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: non-repeat region의 in-frame deletion/insertion 또는 stop-loss variant로 protein length가 변할 때 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • in-frame indel이 repetitive region인지 아닌지 확인한다.
  • 삭제/삽입 위치가 critical domain, structural motif, active site와 관련 있는가?
  • stop-loss가 실제 C-terminal extension을 만들고 기능적으로 의미가 있을 가능성이 있는가?
  • splicing effect가 더 적절한 primary mechanism은 아닌가?

주의: repeat region의 in-frame indel은 benign 방향 BP3 검토 대상일 수 있다.

5.10 PM5: 같은 residue의 다른 missense pathogenic variant

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: 같은 amino acid residue에서 다른 missense change가 pathogenic으로 확립되어 있고, 새 missense variant가 관찰되었을 때 적용한다.

예: p.Arg156His가 pathogenic이고 새 variant가 p.Arg156Cys라면 PM5를 검토한다.

확인할 것:

  • 기존 variant가 충분한 근거로 pathogenic인지 확인한다.
  • residue-level mechanism이 amino acid substitution 종류와 무관하게 중요한지 확인한다.
  • 새 amino acid change가 conservative substitution인지 radical substitution인지 고려한다.
  • splicing effect 차이가 있는지 확인한다.

주의: 같은 residue라고 항상 pathogenic은 아니다. 같은 codon의 synonymous 또는 splicing variant와 혼동하면 안 된다.

5.11 PM6: assumed de novo

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: de novo로 추정되지만 paternity와 maternity가 확인되지 않은 경우 적용한다.

ClinGen 업데이트: PS2와 같이 2018/2021 SVI de novo guidance에서 point-based로 다룬다. confirmed parental relationship이면 PS2 계열, unconfirmed parental relationship이면 PM6 계열로 점수를 낮게 부여한다. phenotype이 gene-disease와 맞지 않으면 점수를 주지 않는다.

5.12 PP1: cosegregation

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: disease를 일으키는 것으로 확립된 gene에서 variant가 여러 affected family members와 함께 segregate할 때 적용한다. segregation data가 많아질수록 더 강한 evidence로 사용할 수 있다고 설명한다.

확인할 것:

  • family members의 phenotype diagnosis가 정확한가?
  • penetrance와 age of onset을 고려했는가?
  • unaffected carrier가 정말 unaffected인지, 아직 발병 전인지 확인했는가?
  • phenocopy 가능성이 있는 흔한 phenotype인지 확인했는가?
  • locus heterogeneity가 큰 질환인지 확인한다.

ClinGen 업데이트: 2023/2024 PP1/BS4/PP4 guidance는 phenotype specificity와 co-segregation을 함께 보는 point-based heuristic을 제안한다. 단순 family count가 아니라 gene-phenotype specificity, locus heterogeneity, segregation information을 통합해야 한다.

5.13 PP2: missense가 주 disease mechanism인 gene

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: benign missense variation rate가 낮고 missense variants가 disease의 흔한 mechanism인 gene에서 missense variant가 발견되면 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • gene-level missense constraint가 disease mechanism과 연결되는가?
  • truncating, splice, CNV가 주 mechanism인 gene은 아닌가?
  • ClinGen/VCEP에서 해당 gene에 PP2 사용을 허용하는가?

주의: 모든 missense variant에 PP2를 붙이면 안 된다. gene-specific mechanism criterion이다.

5.14 PP3: computational evidence가 deleterious effect를 지지

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: conservation, evolutionary evidence, splicing impact 등 여러 computational line이 gene/gene product에 deleterious effect를 지지할 때 적용한다.

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline caveat: 많은 in silico algorithms가 같은 또는 유사한 input을 사용하므로 predictor 여러 개를 독립 evidence처럼 더하면 안 된다. PP3는 한 variant 평가에서 한 번만 쓴다.

현재 권고안:

  • missense는 ClinGen-calibrated tools와 cutoff를 쓰는 것이 안전하다.
  • splicing은 SpliceAI 같은 splicing predictor를 사용할 수 있으나 canonical +/-1,2 splice site는 대개 PVS1 splicing decision tree가 먼저다.
  • population frequency를 score에 포함하는 tool은 PM2/BA1/BS1과 double counting 위험이 있다.
  • VCEP/CSpec이 특정 tool과 cutoff를 지정하면 그 scope 안에서 우선한다.

자세한 cutoff는 아래 PP3/BP4 computational evidence 섹션에 따로 정리했다.

5.15 PP4: highly specific phenotype

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: patient phenotype 또는 family history가 single genetic etiology disease에 매우 특이적이면 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • phenotype이 해당 gene-disease에 얼마나 특이적인가?
  • clinical diagnosis가 객관적 기준으로 확립되었는가?
  • locus heterogeneity가 낮은 disease인가?
  • broad phenotype 또는 nonspecific phenotype을 과대평가하고 있지는 않은가?

ClinGen 업데이트: PP1/BS4/PP4 guidance는 PP4를 segregation evidence와 분리하기 어렵다고 보고, gene-phenotype specificity와 cosegregation을 함께 정량화하는 접근을 제안한다.

5.16 PP5: reputable source가 pathogenic으로 보고

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: reputable source가 variant를 pathogenic으로 보고했지만, laboratory가 독립적으로 evidence를 평가할 수 없을 때 적용한다.

현재 권고안:

  • ClinGen SVI는 PP5/BP6 같은 "근거 없는 reputable source assertion" 사용을 피하는 방향을 권고했다. 근거는 Biesecker and Harrison, 2018 (Genetics in Medicine; DOI 10.1038/gim.2018.42)와 ClinGen General Sequence Variant Curation Process SOP v3.2의 "Reputable source section"이다.
  • ClinVar, HGMD, locus-specific database의 결론만 가져오지 말고 underlying evidence를 직접 criteria로 재분해해야 한다.
  • ClinGen expert panel assertion처럼 evidence가 공개되어 있으면 PP5 대신 해당 evidence code를 검토한다.

실무 메모: 교육용 페이지에서는 PP5/BP6를 "2015 ACMG/AMP guideline에는 있었지만 현재 권고안에서는 독립 code로 쓰지 않는 방향"으로 표시하는 것이 좋다.

5.17 BA1: 너무 높은 allele frequency

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: Exome Sequencing Project, 1000 Genomes, ExAC에서 allele frequency가 5%를 초과하면 benign stand-alone으로 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • gnomAD 등 최신 population DB에서 ancestry-specific frequency가 높은가?
  • variant calling quality와 coverage가 충분한가?
  • disease가 common low-penetrance risk allele인지 Mendelian high-penetrance disease인지 구분한다.
  • founder pathogenic variant나 pseudodeficiency allele처럼 BA1 exception에 해당하는지 확인한다.

ClinGen 업데이트: 2018 BA1 recommendation은 BA1 exception list와 함께 BA1 사용을 구체화했다. MAF > 0.05라도 일부 예외가 있으므로 exception list와 disease context를 확인한다.

5.18 BS1: disease expectation보다 높은 allele frequency

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: allele frequency가 해당 disorder에서 기대 가능한 pathogenic variant frequency보다 높을 때 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • disease prevalence
  • penetrance
  • inheritance model
  • allelic and locus heterogeneity
  • maximum credible allele frequency
  • ancestry-specific frequency

주의: BS1은 단순히 "빈도가 조금 있다"가 아니다. 질환 모델상 pathogenic이라고 보기 어려울 만큼 높아야 한다.

5.19 BS2: 건강 성인에서 관찰

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: full penetrance가 early age에 기대되는 recessive, dominant, X-linked disorder에서 해당 genotype을 가진 healthy adult가 관찰되면 적용한다.

적용 예:

  • recessive disorder: homozygous healthy adult
  • dominant disorder: heterozygous healthy adult
  • X-linked disorder: hemizygous healthy adult

확인할 것:

  • 질환이 정말 early-onset full penetrance인가?
  • 관찰된 사람이 충분히 나이가 많고 phenotype 평가가 되었는가?
  • reduced penetrance, variable expressivity, mild phenotype을 고려했는가?
  • population DB의 "healthy" 여부를 과신하지 않는가?

5.20 BS3: 기능실험이 no damaging effect를 지지

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: well-established functional studies가 protein function 또는 splicing에 damaging effect가 없음을 보여줄 때 적용한다.

ClinGen 업데이트: PS3와 같은 Brnich et al., 2019 (Genome Medicine) guidance를 따른다. assay가 disease mechanism과 맞고, benign/pathogenic controls로 충분히 calibrated 되어야 한다. assay가 해당 기능만 정상임을 보여줄 뿐 disease-relevant mechanism 전체를 배제하지 못하면 strength를 낮추거나 적용하지 않는다.

5.21 BS4: lack of segregation

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: affected family members에서 variant가 disease와 segregate하지 않으면 benign strong evidence로 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • phenocopy 가능성이 높은 질환인가?
  • family에 두 번째 pathogenic variant 또는 다른 locus cause가 있을 수 있는가?
  • penetrance와 age of onset을 고려했는가?
  • 가족 관계와 sample identity가 확인되었는가?

ClinGen 업데이트: PP1/BS4/PP4 guidance는 non-segregation을 formal linkage 또는 point-based heuristic 관점에서 다루도록 제안한다. 단순히 affected 한 명에서 variant가 없다는 이유만으로 BS4를 기계적으로 적용하면 안 된다.

5.22 BP1: truncating이 주 mechanism인 gene에서 missense

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: 주로 truncating variants가 disease를 일으키는 gene에서 missense variant이면 benign supporting evidence로 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • 해당 gene-disease에서 pathogenic missense가 드문가?
  • LOF haploinsufficiency가 주 mechanism인가?
  • missense gain-of-function 또는 dominant-negative mechanism이 알려져 있지 않은가?

주의: "LOF gene이니 missense는 benign"이라고 자동화하면 안 된다. 일부 LOF gene에도 pathogenic missense가 존재한다.

5.23 BP2: phase가 benign 방향을 지지

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의:

  • fully penetrant dominant gene/disorder에서 pathogenic variant와 trans로 관찰되거나
  • 어떤 inheritance pattern에서든 pathogenic variant와 cis로 관찰되면 적용한다.

직관:

  • dominant fully penetrant disease에서 두 pathogenic hits가 trans로 있어도 phenotype이 설명되지 않거나 생존 가능성과 맞지 않으면 benign 방향 근거가 될 수 있다.
  • cis에 이미 pathogenic variant가 있고 phenotype이 그 variant로 설명되면, 평가 variant는 덜 의심된다.

확인할 것:

  • phase가 실험적으로 확인되었는가?
  • 반대 또는 같은 allele의 variant가 정말 pathogenic인가?
  • disease model이 fully penetrant dominant인지 확인한다.

5.24 BP3: repetitive region의 in-frame indel

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: known function이 없는 repetitive region에서 in-frame deletion/insertion이 있으면 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • 해당 repeat region이 low complexity 또는 tolerant region인가?
  • domain, motif, active site, known pathogenic repeat mechanism이 없는가?
  • indel calling quality가 좋은가?

주의: repeat expansion disorder처럼 repeat 자체가 mechanism인 질환에는 단순 적용하면 안 된다.

5.25 BP4: computational evidence가 no impact를 지지

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: conservation, evolutionary, splicing impact 등 computational evidence가 gene 또는 gene product에 영향을 주지 않을 것으로 지지하면 적용한다.

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline caveat: BP4도 PP3처럼 predictor 여러 개를 독립 근거처럼 더하면 안 된다. 한 variant 평가에서 한 번만 사용한다.

현재 권고안:

  • missense는 calibrated benign thresholds를 사용한다.
  • splicing은 SpliceAI lower cutoff 등을 사용할 수 있다.
  • pathogenic direction evidence가 강한데 BP4가 weak하게 나온 경우, evidence conflict와 tool limitation을 검토한다.

자세한 cutoff는 아래 PP3/BP4 섹션에 정리했다.

5.26 BP5: alternate molecular basis

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: 환자의 phenotype을 설명할 수 있는 다른 molecular basis가 있는 case에서 variant가 발견되면 적용한다.

확인할 것:

  • alternate variant가 P/LP이고 phenotype을 충분히 설명하는가?
  • dual diagnosis 가능성이 낮은가?
  • phenotype이 두 질환으로 나뉘어 설명될 수 있지는 않은가?
  • recessive disease에서 한 allele만 설명된 것은 아닌가?

주의: 다른 후보 variant가 있다는 이유만으로 BP5를 적용하면 안 된다. alternate molecular diagnosis가 설득력 있어야 한다.

5.27 BP6: reputable source가 benign으로 보고

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: reputable source가 benign으로 보고했지만 laboratory가 독립적으로 evidence를 평가할 수 없을 때 적용한다.

현재 권고안:

  • PP5와 같이 현재는 독립 criterion으로 쓰지 않는 방향이 권고된다. 근거는 Biesecker and Harrison, 2018 (Genetics in Medicine; DOI 10.1038/gim.2018.42)와 ClinGen General Sequence Variant Curation Process SOP v3.2의 "Reputable source section"이다.
  • ClinVar benign assertion을 그대로 BP6로 쓰기보다 frequency, functional, segregation 등 underlying evidence를 직접 평가한다.

5.28 BP7: splicing 영향 없는 synonymous variant

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline 정의: synonymous variant에서 splicing prediction이 splice consensus sequence에 영향이 없고 새로운 splice site도 만들지 않으며 nucleotide가 highly conserved가 아닐 때 적용한다.

현재 권고안:

  • synonymous만이 아니라 일부 intronic variant에도 VCEP/ClinGen splicing guidance가 BP7-like reasoning을 확장할 수 있다.
  • donor/acceptor splice region, 특히 exon의 first base 또는 last 3 bases처럼 splicing risk가 높은 위치에는 조심한다.
  • missense effect가 함께 있는 variant에는 "splicing 영향 없음"만으로 BP7을 적용해 전체 variant를 benign 쪽으로 밀면 안 된다.

ClinGen splicing guidance:

  • canonical donor/acceptor +/-1,2는 PVS1 splicing decision tree가 우선이다.
  • SpliceAI lower cutoff로 BP4를 적용한 뒤, synonymous/intronic context와 위치 조건을 만족할 때 BP7을 검토한다.

6. PP3/BP4 computational evidence 자세히 보기

PP3/BP4는 실무에서 자주 쓰이지만 오용도 많다. 핵심 원칙은 네 가지다.

  1. 같은 input을 공유하는 predictor들을 여러 개 더해 독립 evidence처럼 세지 않는다.
  2. calibrated cutoff가 있는 tool을 우선한다.
  3. missense prediction과 splicing prediction을 구분한다.
  4. gene/disease-specific VCEP specification이 있으면 해당 specification을 따른다.

6.2 Splicing predictor cutoff 예시

ClinGen SVI Splicing Subgroup guidance는 SpliceAI maximum delta score를 예로 들며 non-canonical splice site variants에서 다음과 같은 decision tree를 제시한다.

canonical +/-1,2 splice site인가?
  예 -> PP3/BP4보다 PVS1 splicing decision tree 먼저 검토
  아니오 -> SpliceAI max delta score 확인
        >=0.20 -> PP3 splicing evidence 검토
        <=0.10 -> BP4 splicing evidence 검토
        0.10-0.20 -> PP3/BP4 splicing evidence 적용하지 않음

주의:

  • SpliceAI >=0.20은 spliceogenicity evidence이며, 그 splicing change가 disease-causing LOF인지까지 자동으로 증명하지 않는다.
  • SpliceAI <=0.10은 no splice impact evidence에 가깝지만, protein-level missense effect를 배제하지 않는다.
  • BP7은 donor/acceptor splice region의 synonymous variant에 보수적으로 적용해야 한다.

ACMG/AMP는 variant-level pathogenicity framework다 gene-disease validity나 patient diagnosis와 섞으면 안 됨
2015 ACMG/AMP guideline framework는 rule-based다 원래는 단순 점수 합산표가 아님

Overview

This English overview is for readers who are new to ACMG/AMP germline sequence variant interpretation. Its main goal is to keep the "official baseline," "current application guidance," and "gene/disease-specific specifications" conceptually separate.

Review date: 2026-05-10.

1. One-sentence summary

ACMG/AMP variant interpretation is a framework for classifying a germline sequence variant as Pathogenic, Likely pathogenic, VUS, Likely benign, or Benign by combining multiple evidence types at predefined strengths.

2. The three layers to understand first

Layer What it is How to use it now Common source of confusion
2015 ACMG/AMP guideline The official published baseline from Richards et al., 2015 (Genetics in Medicine) The core framework for germline sequence variant interpretation It is old, but it remains the publicly verifiable official baseline
ClinGen Variant Classification Guidance ClinGen recommendations for applying 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline criteria more consistently Check criterion-specific application for PVS1, PM2, PS2/PM6, PP3/BP4, PS3/BS3, and others It is not a new final guideline that replaces the entire 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline
ClinGen VCEP/CSpec ACMG/AMP criteria specifications tailored to a specific gene/disease context Prioritize when the gene and disease scope match Do not generalize a gene/disease-specific rule to all variants

3. What ACMG/AMP actually asks

ACMG/AMP classification is not an automated tool for deciding whether "this patient has this disease." The more precise question is:

Does this specific variant, in this specific gene-disease context, have enough evidence to be considered disease-causing?

Therefore, three items must be fixed before classification.

What to fix first Example Why it matters
Variant identity transcript, cDNA, protein notation Evidence differs variant by variant, even within the same gene
Gene-disease context SCN1A and developmental epileptic encephalopathy The same gene can have different disease mechanisms in different disorders
Inheritance model autosomal dominant, autosomal recessive, X-linked Population frequency, segregation, and de novo evidence are interpreted differently

If these three items are unclear, the logic remains unstable even if evidence codes are assigned.

4. The five final classifications

Classification Plain-language meaning Practical interpretation
Pathogenic Disease-causing variant Evidence is sufficient to consider the variant causal
Likely pathogenic Variant likely to be disease-causing The variant is likely causal but not as definitive as pathogenic
VUS Variant of uncertain significance Evidence is insufficient or conflicting. It should not be overinterpreted as a diagnostic conclusion
Likely benign Variant likely not disease-causing The variant is unlikely to be causal
Benign Non-disease-causing variant Evidence is sufficient to consider the variant non-causal

The most important message is VUS. A VUS is not "weakly benign" or "suspicious"; it means classification is deferred because the current evidence is insufficient.

5. Basic structure of evidence codes

ACMG/AMP evidence codes are divided into pathogenic-direction and benign-direction evidence.

Direction Strength Example codes Intuitive meaning
Pathogenic Very Strong PVS1 Very strong evidence such as loss-of-function consistent with the disease mechanism
Pathogenic Strong PS1, PS2, PS3, PS4 Same amino acid change, confirmed de novo, functional evidence, case enrichment, and similar evidence
Pathogenic Moderate PM1-PM6 Hotspot, rarity, in trans observation, protein length change, and similar evidence
Pathogenic Supporting PP1-PP5 Segregation, computational evidence, phenotype specificity, and similar evidence
Benign Stand-alone BA1 Too common to be pathogenic
Benign Strong BS1-BS4 High frequency, healthy adult observations, normal functional evidence, non-segregation, and similar evidence
Benign Supporting BP1-BP7 Weaker supporting evidence in the benign direction

These codes are not a simple score sheet. Each criterion must be checked for applicability, independence from other evidence, and any strength modification in current ClinGen guidance.

The full table of 28 criteria, the 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline combination rules, detailed criterion explanations, and PP3/BP4 cutoff examples are summarized separately in 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline criteria and later updates. Use this overview for the big picture, then use the criteria document to check each application rule in detail.

6. Practical classification workflow

As an educational simplification, ACMG/AMP variant interpretation follows this workflow.

1. Define the variant precisely
2. Check gene-disease validity and inheritance
3. First evaluate benign evidence using population frequency
4. Examine variant consequence and disease mechanism
5. Collect computational, functional, segregation, de novo, and phenotype evidence
6. Assign ACMG/AMP criteria and evidence strengths
7. Check ClinGen general/criterion-specific guidance and VCEP/CSpec availability
8. If an in-scope VCEP/CSpec exists, prioritize that specification
9. Combine evidence to produce a 5-tier classification
10. If the result is VUS, state what evidence is missing

Benign evidence is checked early for a simple reason: if a variant is too common given the disease prevalence and penetrance, it is unlikely to be pathogenic.

7. Common points of confusion

Confusion Correction
The newest file is the official guideline A recent draft and an official final guideline are different

Gene-level evidence is variant-level evidence Gene-disease validity and variant pathogenicity are different

Major evidence domains

1. Population Frequency

Question: Is this variant too common to be a plausible cause of disease?

Check:

  • What is the allele frequency in population databases such as gnomAD?
  • Was the ancestry-matched population examined?
  • Is the frequency allowable after considering disease prevalence, penetrance, and inheritance model?
  • Does it meet benign evidence such as BA1 or BS1?
  • Are you overusing absence from population databases as pathogenic evidence?

Caution: Absence from gnomAD is usually not strong pathogenic evidence. Very rare variants are common in human genomes.

2. Variant Consequence and Gene Mechanism

Question: What effect is the variant expected to have on the protein or splicing?

Examples:

  • nonsense or frameshift
  • canonical splice donor/acceptor
  • missense
  • synonymous with possible splicing effect
  • in-frame insertion/deletion
  • start-loss or stop-loss

The key issue is the gene mechanism. For example, even a truncating variant should receive strong PVS1 only when loss-of-function is an established disease mechanism for that gene-disease pair.

Caution: Do not automatically apply PVS1 to every truncating variant. Check transcript relevance, nonsense-mediated decay, exon position, and gene mechanism.

3. Computational Prediction

Question: What direction do in silico predictors support?

Examples:

  • missense predictors: REVEL, BayesDel, AlphaMissense, and others
  • splicing predictors: SpliceAI and others
  • conservation, protein domain, and structural context

Caution: Computational evidence is usually supporting-level evidence. Do not count several similar predictors as independent evidence.

4. Functional Evidence

Question: Does a functional assay convincingly show disease-relevant loss of function or normal function?

Check:

  • Is the assay directly relevant to the disease mechanism?
  • Was it calibrated with known pathogenic and benign controls?
  • Are thresholds clear and reproducible?
  • Is the evidence strong enough for PS3 or BS3, or only supporting-level?

Caution: A wet-lab result is not automatically strong evidence. Assay validity is the core issue.

5. Segregation and De Novo Evidence

Question: Does the variant track with disease in a family, or did it arise de novo in an affected proband?

Check:

  • Was parentage confirmed?
  • Does the phenotype fit the gene-disease relationship?
  • Were penetrance and age-related expression considered?
  • Are there unaffected carriers?
  • Is there another pathogenic variant or alternate cause?

Caution: "De novo" alone is not enough. Parentage, phenotype specificity, and alternate causes must be checked.

6. Case Observation and Database Evidence

Question: How has this variant been reported in affected individuals, ClinVar, the literature, or expert panels?

Check:

  • ClinVar review status and submitter confidence
  • ClinGen expert panel classification
  • phenotype and transcript consistency
  • date of report and any later reclassification
  • whether the evidence concerns the same variant, the same amino acid change, or merely the same gene

Caution: Do not copy a ClinVar pathogenic assertion without reviewing evidence details and review status.

Intuition for criteria combinations

ACMG/AMP produces the final classification by combining evidence codes. For education, start with these principles:

  • Enough strong pathogenic evidence leads to Pathogenic or Likely pathogenic.
  • Enough strong benign evidence leads to Benign or Likely benign.
  • Insufficient or conflicting evidence usually remains VUS.
  • Stand-alone benign evidence such as BA1 outweighs weak pathogenic signals.
  • Evidence derived from the same information must not be counted multiple times.

In an HTML page, evidence blocks can visually accumulate toward a final class, but the page should not look like an automated diagnostic calculator.

Where ClinGen fits

ClinGen provides guidance and specifications to make the 2015 ACMG/AMP framework more consistent in practice.

A practical and safe order is:

  1. Fix variant identity, gene-disease context, and inheritance model first.
  2. Use the 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline to define the major structure of evidence codes and the 5-tier classification.
  3. Check ClinGen Variant Classification Guidance for general recommendations and criterion-specific recommendations.
  4. Check whether an approved VCEP/CSpec specification exists for the gene/disease pair.
  5. If the scope matches, prioritize the gene/disease-specific VCEP/CSpec rule over general ClinGen guidance.
  6. Record primary evidence for each criterion, plus strength modification, version, and date.
  7. Produce the final tier using the 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline combination rules or an approved VCEP-specific combination rule.

  3. Distinguish later ClinGen guidance from the original 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline.

2. The 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline is a rule-based framework

The 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline is not a simple additive score sheet. It is a rule-based combination framework. Each evidence criterion supports either the pathogenic or benign direction, and evidence strength is divided into Very Strong, Strong, Moderate, Supporting, and Stand-alone. The final classification is determined by predefined combination rules.

Current recommendations and future application directions increasingly use point-based frameworks. Tavtigian et al., 2020 (Human Mutation) explained that the evidence strengths in the 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline can be abstracted into a point system proportional to Bayesian odds, and ClinGen Variant Classification Guidance lists that paper as a ClinGen-endorsed recommendation. The Table 1 footnote in Bergquist et al., 2025 (Genetics in Medicine) also states that the ACMG/AMP guideline is expected to transition to a point-based system.

Three items must be fixed before classification.

What to fix first Example Reason
Variant identity transcript, cDNA, protein, genome build You must know whether you are evaluating the same variant
Gene-disease context SCN1A - developmental epileptic encephalopathy The same gene can have different mechanisms in different disorders
Inheritance model AD, AR, X-linked Population frequency, segregation, de novo evidence, and phase are interpreted differently

The basic decision tree is:

Variant found
  -> Confirm HGVS and transcript
  -> Check gene-disease validity and inheritance
  -> First evaluate BA1/BS1/BS2 using population frequency
  -> Connect variant consequence with disease mechanism
  -> Review consequence-based criteria such as PVS1, PM4, BP3, BP7
  -> Gather functional, computational, segregation, de novo, and phenotype evidence
  -> Check applicability and independence of each criterion
  -> Adjust strength and cutoffs if ClinGen guidance or VCEP/CSpec exists
  -> Determine the final tier using 2015 ACMG/AMP combination rules or an approved gene-specific rule

5. Detailed explanation of each criterion

5.1 PVS1: null variant in a gene where LOF is the disease mechanism

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when there is a null variant, such as nonsense, frameshift, canonical +/-1 or 2 splice site, initiation codon, or single/multiexon deletion, and loss-of-function is a known disease mechanism for the gene.

Check before applying:

  • Is LOF an established disease mechanism for this gene-disease pair?
  • Is the variant located where nonsense-mediated decay is expected?
  • Could it escape NMD because it is in the last exon or within the last 50 bp of the penultimate exon?
  • If a splice variant causes exon skipping, is the skipped exon in-frame and is a critical domain preserved?
  • Is the clinically relevant transcript being interpreted?
  • Is the gene dominated by dominant-negative or gain-of-function mechanisms, making truncation a poor proxy for LOF?

ClinGen update: PVS1 is one of the most elaborated criteria in the 2018 ClinGen SVI recommendation. The key point is that PVS1 should not always be fixed at Very Strong. Depending on variant type, NMD prediction, exon position, critical region, transcript relevance, and gene mechanism, it can be used as PVS1, PVS1_Strong, PVS1_Moderate, PVS1_Supporting, or not met.

Simplified decision tree:

Is this a null-like variant?
  No -> not a PVS1 candidate
  Yes -> is LOF an established mechanism for this gene-disease pair?
        No -> do not apply PVS1
        Yes -> is it likely to trigger NMD or remove a critical protein region?
              Yes -> PVS1 or PVS1_Strong
              Uncertain -> PVS1_Moderate or PVS1_Supporting
              No -> do not apply PVS1, or consider other criteria

5.2 PS1: same amino acid change

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when a different nucleotide change results in the same amino acid change as a variant already established as pathogenic.

Example: if an existing p.Val600Glu is pathogenic, and a different nucleotide change produces the same p.Val600Glu, review PS1.

Check:

  • Confirm that the comparator variant is truly pathogenic or likely pathogenic.
  • The same amino acid change can still have a different splicing impact.
  • Confirm transcript and codon context.
  • If the comparator is a different amino acid change at the same residue, evaluate PM5 rather than PS1.

Splicing update: ClinGen SVI splicing guidance discusses how to think about PS1 when predicted or observed RNA effects are equivalent. In some cases, protein-level sameness is not enough; the equivalence of splicing effects must also be considered.

5.3 PS2: confirmed de novo

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when the patient has the disease, there is no family history, and the variant is de novo with confirmed maternity and paternity.

Check:

  • Was the absence of the variant confirmed in parental samples?
  • Was parental relationship confirmed?
  • Does the phenotype match the gene-disease relationship?
  • Were mosaicism, nonpaternity, egg donation, or embryo transfer considered?
  • Are there multiple independent de novo observations of the same variant?

ClinGen update: PS2/PM6 were specified using a point-based approach in the 2018 recommendation and 2021 v1.1 update. Each de novo observation receives points according to phenotype specificity and confirmed or assumed parental relationship, and the total score determines Supporting, Moderate, Strong, or Very Strong.

Simplified decision tree:

Does the proband phenotype fit the gene-disease relationship?
  No -> do not use PS2/PM6
  Yes -> are both parents variant-negative?
        No or parents not tested -> no de novo evidence
        Yes -> was parental relationship confirmed?
              Yes -> PS2 family
              No -> PM6 family
        Then determine strength from phenotype specificity and number of independent observations

5.4 PS3: functional evidence supports a damaging effect

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when well-established in vitro or in vivo functional studies support a damaging effect on the gene or gene product.

Check:

  • Does the assay directly measure the disease mechanism?
  • Is the variant effect linked to clinical pathogenicity?
  • Are there sufficient known pathogenic and benign controls?
  • Were assay thresholds predefined and reproducible?
  • Does the experiment measure protein function, splicing, expression, localization, or another property?

ClinGen update: Brnich et al., 2019 (Genome Medicine) PS3/BS3 guidance recommends evaluating functional assays in four steps: define the disease mechanism, assess assay class relevance, validate the assay instance, and then apply it to the individual variant. Without sufficient controls and a statistical framework, Strong should be downgraded to Moderate or Supporting.

5.5 PS4: significant enrichment in affected cases

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when variant prevalence is significantly higher in affected individuals than controls. The 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline gives an example of strong evidence when relative risk or odds ratio is greater than 5 and the confidence interval does not include 1. For very rare variants, repeated observations in unrelated patients with absence from controls may be used as moderate evidence.

Check:

  • Are case and control ascertainment appropriate?
  • Was ancestry matching performed?
  • Are phenotype and disease definitions consistent?
  • Were duplicate patients or related family members avoided?
  • Were publication bias and duplicate ClinVar reports checked?

Caution: "Observed in one patient" is not PS4. Statistical comparison or repeated independent observations are needed.

5.6 PM1: mutational hotspot or critical functional domain

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when a variant lies in a mutational hot spot or well-established critical functional domain without benign variation.

Check:

  • Is the domain established by functional assays, structural biology, or curated disease mechanism?
  • Is benign missense variation rare in the same domain?
  • Is residue-level or domain-level constraint connected to the disease?
  • Is "hotspot" being misused to mean simply a site where variants have been reported?

Caution: Do not apply PM1 only because a protein domain has a database name.

5.7 PM2: absent or very rare in population controls

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when a variant is absent from controls such as the Exome Sequencing Project, 1000 Genomes, or ExAC, or when it is at extremely low frequency for a recessive disorder.

Current recommendations:

  • Use larger population databases such as gnomAD.
  • Check ancestry-specific allele frequency.
  • Check coverage, low-complexity regions, and CNV/indel calling limitations.
  • Consider disease prevalence, penetrance, inheritance, and allelic heterogeneity.

ClinGen update: the 2020 SVI PM2 recommendation proposed downgrading PM2 from Moderate to Supporting by default. The reason is that absence or rarity alone often does not provide moderate odds for pathogenicity. Many ClinGen/VCEP specifications therefore use PM2_Supporting.

Caution: "Absent from gnomAD" is a common rare-variant observation. It should not create a pathogenic conclusion by itself.

5.8 PM3: in trans in a recessive disorder

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when the variant under evaluation is observed in trans with another pathogenic variant in a recessive disorder.

Check:

  • Was phase confirmed through parental or offspring testing?
  • Is the opposite-allele variant pathogenic or likely pathogenic?
  • Was the observation made in an affected patient?
  • Distinguish homozygous observation from compound heterozygosity.
  • Do both variants fit the same gene-disease mechanism?

ClinGen update: the 2019 SVI PM3 recommendation proposed a point-based system. Points are assigned per proband based on confirmed in trans status, unknown phase, homozygous occurrence, and classification of the opposite-allele variant; total points determine PM3_Supporting, PM3, PM3_Strong, or PM3_VeryStrong.

5.9 PM4: protein length change

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply to an in-frame deletion/insertion in a non-repeat region or a stop-loss variant that changes protein length.

Check:

  • Is the in-frame indel in a repetitive region?
  • Is the location related to a critical domain, structural motif, or active site?
  • Does the stop-loss plausibly create a functionally meaningful C-terminal extension?
  • Is splicing effect a more appropriate primary mechanism?

Caution: An in-frame indel in a repeat region may be evaluated under benign-direction BP3.

5.10 PM5: different pathogenic missense variant at the same residue

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when a different missense change at the same amino acid residue is established as pathogenic and a new missense variant is observed.

Example: if p.Arg156His is pathogenic and the new variant is p.Arg156Cys, review PM5.

Check:

  • Confirm that the existing variant is pathogenic based on sufficient evidence.
  • Determine whether the residue-level mechanism is important regardless of substitution type.
  • Consider whether the new amino acid change is conservative or radical.
  • Check whether splicing effects differ.

Caution: The same residue is not always pathogenic. Do not confuse this with a synonymous or splicing variant at the same codon.

5.11 PM6: assumed de novo

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when the variant is assumed de novo but paternity and maternity are not confirmed.

ClinGen update: PS2 and PM6 are handled together in the 2018/2021 SVI de novo guidance using a point-based method. Confirmed parental relationship leads to the PS2 family; unconfirmed parental relationship leads to the PM6 family with lower points. If the phenotype does not fit the gene-disease relationship, no points should be assigned.

5.12 PP1: cosegregation

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when the variant cosegregates with disease in multiple affected family members in a gene established to cause the disease. The guideline also states that more segregation data can support stronger evidence.

Check:

  • Are phenotypes in family members accurately diagnosed?
  • Were penetrance and age of onset considered?
  • Is an unaffected carrier truly unaffected, or possibly presymptomatic?
  • Is the phenotype common enough for phenocopies?
  • Does the disease have high locus heterogeneity?

ClinGen update: 2023/2024 PP1/BS4/PP4 guidance proposes a point-based heuristic that considers phenotype specificity and cosegregation together. It is not just a count of family members; gene-phenotype specificity, locus heterogeneity, and segregation information should be integrated.

5.13 PP2: missense is a major disease mechanism in the gene

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when a missense variant is found in a gene with a low rate of benign missense variation and where missense variants are a common disease mechanism.

Check:

  • Is gene-level missense constraint connected to the disease mechanism?
  • Is the gene instead primarily driven by truncating, splice, or CNV mechanisms?
  • Does ClinGen/VCEP allow use of PP2 for this gene?

Caution: Do not apply PP2 to every missense variant. It is a gene-specific mechanism criterion.

5.14 PP3: computational evidence supports a deleterious effect

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when multiple computational lines, such as conservation, evolutionary evidence, or splicing impact, support a deleterious effect on the gene or gene product.

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline caveat: many in silico algorithms use the same or similar inputs, so several predictors must not be counted as independent evidence. PP3 should be used only once per variant evaluation.

Current recommendations:

  • For missense variants, use ClinGen-calibrated tools and cutoffs when possible.
  • For splicing, predictors such as SpliceAI may be used, but canonical +/-1,2 splice-site variants usually require the PVS1 splicing decision tree first.
  • Tools that include population frequency in the score create double-counting risk with PM2/BA1/BS1.
  • If a VCEP/CSpec specifies a tool and cutoff, prioritize it within that scope.

Detailed cutoffs are summarized below in the PP3/BP4 computational evidence section.

5.15 PP4: highly specific phenotype

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when the patient phenotype or family history is highly specific for a disease with a single genetic etiology.

Check:

  • How specific is the phenotype to this gene-disease relationship?
  • Was the clinical diagnosis established by objective criteria?
  • Is the disease low in locus heterogeneity?
  • Are you overvaluing a broad or nonspecific phenotype?

ClinGen update: PP1/BS4/PP4 guidance notes that PP4 can be difficult to separate from segregation evidence and proposes an approach that quantifies gene-phenotype specificity and cosegregation together.

5.16 PP5: reputable source reports pathogenicity

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when a reputable source reports the variant as pathogenic but the laboratory cannot independently evaluate the evidence.

Current recommendations:

  • ClinGen SVI recommended avoiding "reputable source assertion without evidence" criteria such as PP5/BP6. The supporting sources are Biesecker and Harrison, 2018 (Genetics in Medicine; DOI 10.1038/gim.2018.42) and the "Reputable source section" in ClinGen General Sequence Variant Curation Process SOP v3.2.
  • Do not simply copy conclusions from ClinVar, HGMD, or locus-specific databases. Reconstruct the underlying evidence as ACMG/AMP criteria.
  • If evidence is public, as in a ClinGen expert panel assertion, evaluate the underlying evidence codes instead of using PP5.

Practical note: In this educational page, PP5/BP6 should be shown as criteria included in the 2015 ACMG/AMP guideline but currently discouraged as independent codes.

5.17 BA1: allele frequency is too high

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply benign stand-alone evidence when allele frequency exceeds 5% in resources such as the Exome Sequencing Project, 1000 Genomes, or ExAC.

Check:

  • Is ancestry-specific frequency high in current population databases such as gnomAD?
  • Are variant calling quality and coverage sufficient?
  • Is this a common low-penetrance risk allele rather than a Mendelian high-penetrance disease variant?
  • Is the variant an exception such as a founder pathogenic variant or pseudodeficiency allele?

ClinGen update: the 2018 BA1 recommendation specified BA1 use together with a BA1 exception list. Even if MAF > 0.05, check the exception list and disease context.

5.18 BS1: allele frequency exceeds disease expectation

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when allele frequency is greater than expected for a pathogenic variant in the disorder.

Check:

  • disease prevalence
  • penetrance
  • inheritance model
  • allelic and locus heterogeneity
  • maximum credible allele frequency
  • ancestry-specific frequency

Caution: BS1 does not mean "some frequency exists." The frequency must be high enough that pathogenicity is implausible under the disease model.

5.19 BS2: observed in a healthy adult

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when a healthy adult is observed with the relevant genotype for a recessive, dominant, or X-linked disorder expected to have full penetrance at an early age.

Examples:

  • recessive disorder: homozygous healthy adult
  • dominant disorder: heterozygous healthy adult
  • X-linked disorder: hemizygous healthy adult

Check:

  • Is the disorder truly early-onset and fully penetrant?
  • Is the observed individual old enough and phenotypically evaluated?
  • Were reduced penetrance, variable expressivity, and mild phenotype considered?
  • Are you overtrusting "healthy" labels in population databases?

5.20 BS3: functional evidence supports no damaging effect

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when well-established functional studies show no damaging effect on protein function or splicing.

ClinGen update: follow the same Brnich et al., 2019 (Genome Medicine) PS3/BS3 guidance. The assay must match the disease mechanism and be calibrated with benign/pathogenic controls. If the assay only shows one function is normal but cannot rule out the disease-relevant mechanism overall, downgrade the strength or do not apply.

5.21 BS4: lack of segregation

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply benign strong evidence when a variant does not segregate with disease in affected family members.

Check:

  • Is the disease prone to phenocopy?
  • Could there be a second pathogenic variant or another locus cause in the family?
  • Were penetrance and age of onset considered?
  • Were family relationships and sample identity confirmed?

ClinGen update: PP1/BS4/PP4 guidance recommends treating non-segregation using formal linkage or a point-based heuristic. Do not mechanically apply BS4 only because one affected individual does not carry the variant.

5.22 BP1: missense in a gene where truncating variants are the main mechanism

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply benign supporting evidence when the variant is missense in a gene where disease is primarily caused by truncating variants.

Check:

  • Are pathogenic missense variants rare for this gene-disease relationship?
  • Is LOF haploinsufficiency the main mechanism?
  • Are missense gain-of-function or dominant-negative mechanisms known?

Caution: Do not automate "LOF gene means missense is benign." Some LOF genes still have pathogenic missense variants.

5.23 BP2: phase supports a benign interpretation

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition:

  • apply when the variant is observed in trans with a pathogenic variant in a fully penetrant dominant gene/disorder, or
  • when it is observed in cis with a pathogenic variant under any inheritance pattern.

Intuition:

  • In a fully penetrant dominant disorder, two pathogenic hits in trans may not fit the phenotype or viability expectation and can support benign interpretation.
  • If a pathogenic variant in cis already explains the phenotype, the variant being evaluated becomes less suspicious.

Check:

  • Was phase experimentally confirmed?
  • Is the opposite-allele or same-allele variant truly pathogenic?
  • Is the disease model fully penetrant dominant?

5.24 BP3: in-frame indel in a repetitive region

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when there is an in-frame deletion/insertion in a repetitive region without known function.

Check:

  • Is the repeat region low-complexity or tolerant?
  • Is there no domain, motif, active site, or known pathogenic repeat mechanism?
  • Is indel calling quality good?

Caution: Do not apply this simplistically to repeat expansion disorders where the repeat itself is the disease mechanism.

5.25 BP4: computational evidence supports no impact

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when computational evidence such as conservation, evolutionary evidence, or splicing impact supports no effect on the gene or gene product.

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline caveat: as with PP3, multiple predictors should not be counted as independent evidence. Use BP4 only once per variant evaluation.

Current recommendations:

  • For missense variants, use calibrated benign thresholds.
  • For splicing, use thresholds such as the SpliceAI lower cutoff.
  • If strong pathogenic-direction evidence exists but BP4 is weakly met, review evidence conflict and tool limitations.

Detailed cutoffs are summarized in the PP3/BP4 section below.

5.26 BP5: alternate molecular basis

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when the variant is found in a case with another molecular basis that can explain the patient's phenotype.

Check:

  • Is the alternate variant P/LP and sufficient to explain the phenotype?
  • Is dual diagnosis unlikely?
  • Could the phenotype be split across two disorders?
  • In a recessive disease, is only one allele explained?

Caution: Do not apply BP5 merely because another candidate variant exists. The alternate molecular diagnosis must be convincing.

5.27 BP6: reputable source reports benignity

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when a reputable source reports the variant as benign but the laboratory cannot independently evaluate the evidence.

Current recommendations:

  • As with PP5, current recommendations discourage using this as an independent criterion. The supporting sources are Biesecker and Harrison, 2018 (Genetics in Medicine; DOI 10.1038/gim.2018.42) and the "Reputable source section" in ClinGen General Sequence Variant Curation Process SOP v3.2.
  • Instead of using a ClinVar benign assertion as BP6, evaluate the underlying evidence directly, such as frequency, functional evidence, or segregation.

5.28 BP7: synonymous variant with no splicing impact

2015 ACMG/AMP guideline definition: apply when a synonymous variant has no predicted effect on the splice consensus sequence, does not create a new splice site, and the nucleotide is not highly conserved.

Current recommendations:

  • BP7-like reasoning can be extended to some intronic variants under VCEP/ClinGen splicing guidance, not only synonymous variants.
  • Be cautious around donor/acceptor splice regions, especially the first base of an exon or the last three bases of an exon.
  • If a variant also has a missense effect, do not use "no splicing impact" alone to push the whole variant toward benign.

ClinGen splicing guidance:

  • For canonical donor/acceptor +/-1,2 variants, the PVS1 splicing decision tree comes first.
  • After applying BP4 using a SpliceAI lower cutoff, review BP7 if the synonymous/intronic context and position conditions are met.

6. PP3/BP4 computational evidence in detail

PP3/BP4 are commonly used in practice and commonly misused. The four core principles are:

  1. Do not add several predictors with shared inputs as independent evidence.
  2. Prefer tools with calibrated cutoffs.
  3. Separate missense prediction from splicing prediction.
  4. Follow gene/disease-specific VCEP specifications when they exist.

6.2 Splicing predictor cutoff example

ClinGen SVI Splicing Subgroup guidance uses SpliceAI maximum delta score as an example and presents a decision tree for non-canonical splice-site variants.

Is this a canonical +/-1,2 splice-site variant?
  Yes -> review the PVS1 splicing decision tree before PP3/BP4
  No -> check SpliceAI max delta score
        >=0.20 -> review PP3 splicing evidence
        <=0.10 -> review BP4 splicing evidence
        0.10-0.20 -> do not apply PP3/BP4 splicing evidence

Caution:

  • SpliceAI >=0.20 is evidence of spliceogenicity, not automatic proof that the splicing change causes disease-causing LOF.
  • SpliceAI <=0.10 is close to evidence for no splice impact, but it does not rule out protein-level missense effects.
  • BP7 should be applied conservatively to synonymous variants near donor/acceptor splice regions.
